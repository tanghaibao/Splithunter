Interpretation

  • Strong, statistically clean dip. Focal median depth (1) is exactly half the baseline median (2), giving log2R = -1.0 and an estimated 50% T-cell fraction. The CI is bounded at 0.5 from above because the focal depth cannot drop below 1 read in this exome dataset.
  • Biological mechanism. Mature T-cells delete one allele of TCRA during V-J recombination; in a sample where ~50% of cells are T-cells, ~25% of TCRA-locus DNA is missing at the deletion midpoint, yielding the focal coverage drop visible above. With somatic copy-number heterogeneity this lower-bound is what TcellExTRECT calibrates against.
  • Contrast with NA12878 (TRA splithunter report). The NA12878 LCL slice in tests/NA12878.bam shows focal/baseline medians of 29/31 (log₂R ≈ -0.10, fraction ≈ 6% with CI [0%, 71%]) — i.e. no real signal, as expected for an EBV-immortalized B-cell line. This cov_example sample is the contrasting positive control: unmistakable T-cell infiltrate with a tight coordinate dip.

Reproduce

python -c "
import csv, gzip
from splithunter import _core
segs = {r['segName']: (int(r['start']), int(r['end']))
         for r in csv.DictReader(open('tests/fixtures/tcra_seg_hg19.csv'))}
pos, dep = [], []
for r in csv.DictReader(gzip.open('tests/fixtures/cov_example.csv.gz', 'rt')):
    pos.append(int(r['pos'])); dep.append(int(r['reads']))
print(dict(_core.fraction_from_coverage(
    pos, dep,
    segs['focal'][0], segs['focal'][1],
    segs['local1'][0], segs['local1'][1],
    segs['local2'][0], segs['local2'][1], 0)))
